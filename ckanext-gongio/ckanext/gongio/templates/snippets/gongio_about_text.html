Gong.io is an open data initiative and platform for Wollongong, Shellharbour, Kiama and the greater Illawarra region (an hour south of Sydney, Australia).

What is the purpose?

We're on a mission to rapidly transform the Illawarra region through the power of open data, and by harnessing the creativity of makers and developers.

What is open data?

Open data is just as it sounds: it is the idea that certain data should be freely available to everyone to use and republish as they wish, without restrictions.

What is this website?

The website is running free and opensource software called CKAN. It allows individuals to register accounts and upload/share datasets, and also allows busineses, government groups and other organisations the ability to establish a page on the site where they can share and manage their datasets. The CKAN software is safe and secure, and already powers the Data.gov.au and Data.nsw open data platforms.

Who's behind this?

Gong.io was built and launched by Nathan Waters, creator of Hackagong and GongStartups. The initiative is a grassroots effort run by volunteers and managed by the GongStartups community.

Can I help improve the website?

The customised CKAN template code is opensource and available on GitHub. Feel free to fork it and send me pull requests!
